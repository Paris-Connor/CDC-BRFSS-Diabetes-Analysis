# CDC BRFSS 2023 Diabetes Risk Factor Analysis

A public health data analysis project using the CDC's 2023 Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) dataset. This project examines how diabetes prevalence varies across demographics and different health factors by cleaning, analyzing, and visualizing survey data using Python.

---

## Project Overview

Diabetes is one of the most common chronic diseases in the United States and is associated with many long term health complications. This project analyzes data from the CDC's 2023 Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) to examine relationships between diabetes prevalence and factors such as age, sex, obesity status, and BMI.

This project demonstrates the complete data analysis workflow of importing raw data, cleaning and validating survey responses, transforming coded variables into readable categories, performing descriptive statistical analysis, creating visualizations, and communicating findings through documentation and a written report.

---

## Objectives

- Clean and validate raw CDC BRFSS survey data
- Prepare an analysis-ready dataset
- Explore diabetes prevalence across demographic and health factors
- Produce descriptive statistics and visualizations
- Document the entire analysis workflow using reproducible methods

## Key Findings

- Diabetes prevalence increased significantly with age, rising from approximately **1.5%** among adults aged 18–29 to over **20%** among adults aged 60 and older.
- Obese respondents reported diabetes at more than **twice the rate** of non-obese respondents (22.24% vs. 10.23%).
- Men reported a slightly higher diabetes prevalence (14.51%) than women (13.23%), although the difference was relatively small.
- Diabetes prevalence increased consistently across BMI categories, reinforcing the relationship between excess body weight and diabetes.
- The findings align with established public health research highlighting age and obesity as significant factors associated with diabetes prevalence.

Project Overview

This project analyzes over 430,000 responses from the CDC's 2023 Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) to investigate relationships between diabetes prevalence and factors including age, sex, obesity status, and BMI category. The project demonstrates an end-to-end data analysis workflow including data cleaning, validation, exploratory analysis, visualization, and technical reporting using Python.

Key Findings

Age

Diabetes prevalence increased steadily with age, reaching its highest levels among adults over 60.

Obesity

Obese respondents reported diabetes at more than twice the rate of non-obese respondents.

BMI

Diabetes prevalence increased consistently across BMI categories.
